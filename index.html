03

Traffic

Where messages come from.

~80%

Mass Messages

The main traffic source. Sent regularly. Creates replies.

You don't decide what the mass message looks like — but you must keep track and make sure only one is active at a time.

~20%

Automations

Smart Messages and AutoOnlineMessages — see the next section.

Mass Message Rules

01
Only ONE active — that doesn't include @, a price, or a link.
02
Unsending: only unsend previous engagement messages (keep one active) — those are the ones fans have already received and replied to.
03
Never unsend messages with a price, a link, or an @ tag — those always stay.
04
If you're ever unsure — double-check with a supervisor before taking action.
⛔

NEVER unsend AutoPPV

Pay attention to PPV messages with a price that doesn't end on a round number (e.g. 22.35).

Do not unsend those. They generate money for the model — unsending them is forbidden.

04

Automations

Two types — pick the right one for the moment.

AOM

AutoOnlineMessages

Steady

  • Can be scheduled.
  • Lets you select many groups.
  • Messages spread over time — slower than SMs.
SM

SmartMessages

Instant

  • Sends immediately.
  • Spikes your unreads.
  • Hourly cap.
  • Can't be scheduled.

AOMs — Setup Guidelines

📅 Scheduling

No campaign longer than 2 hours.

8-hour shift → 4 campaigns.
12-hour shift → 6 campaigns.

🎯 Target

Always include:

  • All spenders
  • Online fans

Always exclude: muted users.

📎 Content

The caption must match the image.

Always include a question to encourage interaction and keep things fun.

SMs — Setup Guidelines

⏱ Timing

No campaign longer than 2 hours.

You can't schedule them — must be sent manually.

🎯 Target

Include: online fans.

Exclude: muted users.

📎 Content

Caption must match the image.

Always include a question to keep it fun.

05

Binds & Free-typing

Pre-written messages with shortcuts — and when to use them.

What is a bind?

A bind is an automatic message that loads when you press a keybind.

Example: pressing a1 drops a message into the chat area.

When to use them

  • High traffic → use binds to clear messages fast.
  • Low traffic → rely on free-typing.

Filtering Fans

How to read fan info and decide who's worth your time.

Fan Info Breakdown

Subscription time — how long the fan has been subscribed.
Messages — how much the fan spent unlocking messages.
Last activity — most recent subscription, tip, or message unlock.
Total agency spent — total spent across all models in the agency.

3 Main Types of Fans

🐋

Whales

Spent more than $100

Fully personalized chatting. Avoid binds — free-type only. Play into their kinks. Don't try to land one giant sale; warm them up.

  • Set the tone of the convo
  • Tease toward the bundle
  • Set up the bundle
  • Locked message $19–29 — test their mood to spend
💸

Spenders

Spent less than $100

Potential whales. Warm them up, slightly personalize, use names. Don't spam binds — adjust your tone, play into their kinks.

  • Use binds in high-traffic moments — try to free-type
  • Slightly personalize messages
  • Set up the bundle
  • Locked message $14–25
🪫

Time-Wasters

Spent $0 · Low value

Signs: 6+ months subbed, 0 agency spent, asking weird questions. Walk them through your 3-stepper, then nail the sale — rushing never works.

  • Binds (walk them through the 3-stepper)
  • Locked message $14
  • Offer presents — make it sound like a deal

Before You Reply — Ask Yourself

"Is this worth my time?"
